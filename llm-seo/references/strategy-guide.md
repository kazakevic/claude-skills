# LLM SEO Strategy Guide (2026)

## 90-Day Implementation Roadmap

### Weeks 1-2: Audit & Foundation
1. Run AI visibility audit: query ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI with 20-30 non-branded prompts
2. Audit robots.txt: ensure GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot are not blocked
3. Implement llms.txt at domain root
4. Audit brand consistency across all platforms — align bios, descriptions, positioning
5. Check all key pages for semantic HTML, heading hierarchy, alt text

### Weeks 3-6: Content & Schema
1. Add FAQPage, Article, Organization, Person schema to priority pages
2. Restructure top content into chunk-based architecture (one idea per section, answer-first)
3. Add "Last updated" timestamps to all content pages
4. Create 3-5 original research pieces with proprietary data
5. Build FAQ sections with natural-language H2/H3 question headings
6. Create one YouTube how-to video for a high-volume question

### Weeks 7-12: Authority Building
1. Launch earned media campaign: target 3-5 placements in industry publications
2. Pursue inclusion in category roundup and comparison articles
3. Begin genuine Reddit/forum participation with expert answers
4. Update Wikipedia page (if applicable) with current, well-sourced info
5. Establish content refresh cadence: high-priority pages every 2-3 days
6. Re-run AI visibility audit and measure Share of Voice changes

## Off-Page Strategy

### Earned Media (Highest ROI)
- AI engines cite third-party publications 6.5x more than brand sites
- 85.5% of AI citations come from earned media (Forbes, TechCrunch, WSJ, etc.)
- Each placement compounds into dozens of AI citations across platforms
- Prioritize: expert guest posts, industry roundup inclusion, press coverage, comparison reviews

### Wikipedia
- Single most cited source for ChatGPT
- If company meets notability guidelines, ensure page is accurate, comprehensive, well-sourced
- Use third-party citations only (Wikipedia policy)

### Community Presence
- Reddit/Quora: genuine participation with detailed expert answers (heavily cited by Perplexity)
- YouTube: short how-to videos (heavily cited by Google AI Overviews)
- LinkedIn: thought leadership for B2B visibility
- Podcasts, interviews: consistent brand voice builds cross-reference signals

### Brand Consistency
AI models cross-check dozens of sources simultaneously. Inconsistency = confusion = no citation.
- Same brand bio across all platforms
- Consistent value proposition in all interviews, articles, press
- Detailed, current About page matching external profiles
- Organization + Person schema anchoring brand identity

### Backlink Strategy
- Quality over quantity: higher authority tiers get more AI citations
- Diverse referring domains > multiple links from same source
- Image-based links (charts, infographics, data visualizations) perform exceptionally well

## Measurement Framework

### Key Metrics
- **Share of Model (SoM):** Frequency of AI recommendation for relevant queries
- **Citation rate:** % of relevant AI queries citing your content
- **LLM perception drift:** Month-to-month changes in AI brand perception
- **AI-referred traffic:** Visitors from ChatGPT/Perplexity in analytics
- **Branded homepage traffic correlation:** Rising homepage visits + LLM presence = causal signal

### Manual Audit Process
1. Create panel of 20-30 non-branded prompts relevant to your category
2. Run across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews monthly (incognito mode)
3. Track: mentioned? cited with link? which competitors appeared? source types cited?
4. Calculate Share of Voice: % of queries where you appear

### Emerging Tools (2026)
- Evertune: AI brand score tracking across LLMs
- Semrush: expanding into AI visibility metrics
- Manual prompt testing remains most reliable baseline
- No vendor has complete visibility yet — treat data as directional
